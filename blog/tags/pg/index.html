##PROCESSES AND REGULATIONS

The organizational structure of academic administration of IIT Jammu comprises of the following levels starting from the highest one to the gradually decreasing order:

A. Level I: Senate
B. Level II: Senate post graduate board (SPGB)
C. Level III: Department post graduate committee (DPGC)
D. Level IV: Student Research Committee (SRC)

A. Senate It is the highest level administrative body that deals with all academic matters of IIT Jammu. A.1 Chairperson of the Senate The Director of the institute is the chairperson of the Senate.

B. Senate post graduate board (SPGB) It is the level II Senate body, related to all the PG academic matters of the institute.

B.1 Purview of the SPGB

This intermediate body establishes the connection between the Senate and all departments of the institute.

1. The SPGB deals with an academic matter related to M.Tech Students and Ph.D research scholars when it is forwarded and/or recommended by the department post-graduate committee (DPGC).
2. The SPGB can also work when being directed by the Senate.
3. In case of a new proposals for any kind of course or curriculum which have prefarably been forwarded by the DPGC, the SPGB will work on those. The SPGB needs to provide its recommendation to the Senate.
4. Students can appeal and/or approach to the SPGB through proper channel.
5. SPGB needs to resolve the issue/s and take decision/s accordingly. The decision/s need/s to be communicated to the student or the concerned person/s and also to the DPGC.
6. In case, SPGB can‚Äôt resolve the academic issue or there is no clarity on the issue, the case might be forwarded to the Senate. It is recommended that SPGB should avoid forwarding the cases unless there is the need to change the academic policy of the institute by the Senate.
7. All PG agenda items for Senate should be proposed by SPGB including the agenda items proposed by the DPGC.
8. There should be a SPGB meeting every alternate month.

B.2 Chairperson of the SPGB

The chairperson of the SPGB will, generally, be the Dean (academics). Under special circumstance, the chairperson of the SPGB might be different from the Dean (academics) if nominated by the Director.

B.3 Delegation of responsibilities between the chairperson of the SPGB and the Dean academics if they are different

The responsibilities between the chairperson of the SPGB and the Dean (academics) will be delegated as follows if they are different:

1. The institute academic programme will be run and administered under the leadership of the Dean (academics).
2. The academic policy will be framed by the Dean (academics).
3. In absence of the Dean (academics), the Associate Dean (academics) will take charge.
4. The SPGB will monitor the institute academic policy framed by the Dean (academics), redressing the complaints of the students, and deciding on the disciplinary action/s against the students if required or recommending the disciplinary action/s for the approval from the Senate. The issues in

the prerogative of the SPGB will be monitored by the chairperson of the SPGB.

B.4 Members of the SPGB

The SPGB will be constituted by the following members:

1. Chairperson of the SPGB
2. Dean and Associate Deans (academics) as ex-officio members
3. Chairpersons of all DPGCs
4. Institute faculty members nominated by the Director of the institute
5. Two student representatives: To deal with the academic issues of the M.Tech students, one student representative from the first year batch and the other representative from the second year batch should be nominated by the Senate of the students. At the time of discussions related to the academic issues of the M.Tech students, student representatives will be invited for the SPGB meetings

B.5 Convener of the SPGB

The Deputy Registrar (DR) (academics) will be the convener of the SPGB. In absence of the DR (academics), the Assistant Registrar (AR) (academics) will officiate as the convener of the SPGB. It is to be noticed that the convener of the SPGB is not the member of the SPGB. The convener of the SPGB will act as the secretary who will take care of the records, draft the minutes of meetings and perform other necessary official activities.

B.6 Rotation of the members of the SPGB

The members of the SPGB will be rotated as follows:

1. The tenure of faculty members, except the ex-officio members, in the SPGB will be for 2 years.
2. After every year, 50 % of the faculty members in the SPGB will be replaced by the new faculty members.
3. New student representative/s will be inducted every year. To provide fair chance to all students, a student who has acted as student representative earlier will not be allowed to hold this position again in future.

C. Department post graduate committee (DPGC)

It is the level III entity, related to academic matters, of the institute. There will be DPGC for all departments.

C.1 Purview of the DPGC

This intermediate body establishes the connection between the SPGB and students of that particular department.

1. All academic issues, related to the M.Tech programme, of the department need to be routed through the DPGC.
2. Any new proposals for any kind of course or curriculum, are to be dealt by the DPGC. In consultation with the departmental faculty members, the DPGC might formulate a strategy to work on the proposals before providing the recommendation of the DPGC.
3. DPGC can formulate new set of required strategy and guidelines, in concurrence to the policy set by the Senate, to run and administer the M.Tech programme of the department.
4. It is expected that the students first will approach the DPGC in case the issue/s is/are not being solved by the CI, CC, FAs, or the Head of department.
5. It is also expected that the Head of department might refer an academic related case to the DPGC for deliberations.
6. DPGC needs to resolve the issue/s and take decision/s accordingly. The decision/s need/s to be communicated to the student or the concerned person/s.
7. In case, DPGC can‚Äôt resolve the academic issue, the case should be forwarded to the SPGB. It is recommended that DPGC should avoid forwarding the cases unless unavoidable.
8. In the event of academic malpractices and the case is reported to the DPGC, appropriate disciplinary actions need to be recommended by the DPGC and those recommendations are to be submitted to the Dean (academics).
9. All departmental PG agenda items for Senate should be proposed by DPGC and those should be handed over to SPGB.
10. There should be a DPGC meeting every month.

C.2 Chairperson of the DPGC

The chairperson of the DPGC will be nominated by the Dean (academics) in consultation with the Director.

C.3 Delegation of responsibilities between the chairperson of the DPGC and the Head of department The responsibilities between the chairperson of the DPGC and the Head of department will be delegated as follows:

1. The department academic programme will be run and administered under the leadership of the Head of department.
2. The DPGC will act on the department academic policy matters, redressing the complaints of the department students, and deciding on the disciplinary action/s against the students if required or recommending the disciplinary action/s for the approval of the SPGB. The issues in the prerogative of the DPGC will be monitored by the chairperson of the DPGC.

C.4 Members of the DPGC

The DPGC will be constituted by the following members:

1. Convener of the DPGC
2. One faculty member, outside the department, who will be nominated by the Dean (academics) in consultation with the director
3. Head of the department as ex-officio member
4. Three departmental faculty members, of different specialization or significantly different areas of research, who will be nominated by the faculty members of the department
5. Two departmental student representatives: To deal with the departmental academic issues of the M.Tech students, one departmental student representative from the first year M.Tech batch and the other representative from the second year M.Tech batch should be nominated by the Senate of the students. At the time of discussions related to the academic issues of the M.Tech. students, student representatives will be invited for the DPGC meetings.

C.5 Rotation of the members of the DPGC

The members of the DPGC will be rotated as follows:

1. The tenure of faculty members, except the ex-officio members, in the DPGC will be for 2 years.
2. After every year, 50 % of the faculty members in the DPGC will be replaced by the new faculty members.
3. New departmental student representative/s will be inducted every year. To provide fair chance to all departmental students, a student who has acted as departmental student representative earlier will not be allowed to hold this position again in future.

D. Student's Research Committee (SRC):

It is the level IV entity, related to academic matters, of the student. There will be SRC for all students in the departments.

D1. Convener of SRC

The Supervisor of the student is the SRC convener. D2. Members of the SRC

(a) Supervisor(s) of the student is the SRC convener
(b) One expert in the field from the Department;
(c) One Institute faculty expert, preferably in the concerned area, from outside the Department but from IIT Jammu to which the student belongs; and Experts at (b) and (c) above will be nominated by DPGC from amongst those proposed by Supervisor(s).

R.5 REGISTRATION

R.5.1 Every student/candidate will be required to renew the registration every semester till the submission of the Doctoral thesis. The renewal of registration every semester shall be subject to completion of specified number of credits/courses and/or satisfactory progress in his research work as recommended by the DPGC.

R.5.2 A student/candidate who fails to register or renew his registration within 6 months, as the case may be, will cease to be a student/candidate with immediate effect.

R.6 COURSE WORK

R.6.1 Each student will be required to take course work as prescribed by the supervisor(s) and approved by the DPGC.
R.6.2 A student having B.Tech. or equivalent degree shall be required to complete a minimum of 24 credits while M.Tech./M.Sc.(& M.A.) or equivalent degree holder shall be required to complete a minimum of 12 credits of course work. If student with MSc. Degree wants register for PhD (or vice-versa) in Engineering he/she must complete 15 credits of coursework. These courses may be prescribed from the existing M.Tech. and/or pre-Ph.D. courses at the Institute. In addition, a candidate shall be required to take two credit earning courses: a 2 credit course on "Technical Writing and Communication Skills" (course code: HUL910/CCL001P2X) and a 2 credit course on ‚ÄúSoftware tools‚Äù (related to the branch specific, may takes any course from the list of course code CCL101P2X to CCL115P2X). It is expected that full-time, and off campus students will complete their course requirements within three semesters from the date of joining the programme. It is further expected that they will qualify the Comprehensive examinations soon after completing the course requirement but in no case beyond 24 months.
R.6.2.1 All Ph.D. students are to compulsorily register in Course No. DT 900 (Doctoral Thesis) at the time of registration/renewal of registration for the Ph.D. Research work till they submit the Ph.D. thesis, after clearing the comprehensive examination.
R.6.3 The minimum CGPA requirement shall be 7.0 on a 10-point scale for admission to the candidacy of Ph.D. Degree. If the SGPA of any student is below 7.0 at the end of any semester, his/her registration will be terminated with immediate effect and is allowed for appeal to SPGB. If a student will score equal or more than 7.0 SGPA but scored F grade in one/more subject/s he/she, may be given only one chance to clear the subject within 12 months.
R 6.4 A student can transfer his/her credits from other Institute/ Universities, if there is an MoU signed between IIT Jammu and the corresponding Institute/ Universities.
R.6.5 A Full Time Externally funded students who has completed course work/s before getting admission to PhD shall be considered valid if (s)he gets enrolled for PhD program in continuation. The course work need to be completed within last two years from IIT Jammu and shall largely overlap with the institute approved course work structure.

R.7 Ph.D. THESIS SUPERVISOR(S)

R.7.1 Every admitted student shall be assigned a Research Supervisor(s) by the Head of the Department on the recommendations of the DPGC taking into consideration the preference of the student, subject to the approval of the SPGB. For this purpose, the form given in Annexure A will be completed and processed within 10 days after completing the first semester and before starting of the 2nd semester.
R.7.2 A Supervisor(s) can be any regular faculty member including scientific staff of the Institute with a Ph.D. degree. A visiting faculty can only be joint supervisor of a student and once (s)he will be regularized, with the consent of the other supervisor(s) and student; (s)he can be converted to the Supervisor.
R.7.3 The SPGB on the recommendations of the DPGC, may appoint joint Supervisor(s) not exceeding a total of three to supervise the student/Candidate. These may be from inside or outside the Institute and normally, there should not be more than two supervisors from within the Institute. If the Joint- Supervisor is from India, he/she needs to visit once in a semester to IIT Jammu or may be available through video conferencing/in person. If the Joint-Supervisor is from outside India, he/she will be the member of the SRC but his/her presence at Institute for any meeting may not be necessary. Appointment of any Joint Supervisor(s) would not be made after a lapse of 18 calendar months from the date of initial registration of the student/candidate except when none of the supervisors is in the Institute for a period of a year or more at a stretch. The Joint-Supervisor must be proposed by the supervisor and approved by the DPGC/SPGB.
R.7.4 Appointment of Supervisors/Joint-Supervisors and Caretaker-Supervisor for Ph.D. students

A faculty member appointed as a Ph.D. supervisor is normally expected to be available to a research scholar in the Institute till the Ph.D. thesis defense. However, under unavoidable circumstances, such as: long leave of more than 12 months; resignation; retirement; or death; a supervisor may not be available to the scholar. In such special cases, appointment of supervisor(s) will be regulated as under:

I. A supervisor proceeding on long leave of more than 12 months
(a) (i) Where joint-supervisor exists, the supervisor proceeding on long leave for more than 12 months can continue to be a Joint-supervisor provided the DPGC is convinced of effective supervision by the Joint-Supervisor.
(ii) Where a Joint-Supervisor does not exist, a Joint-Supervisor may be appointed by the DPGC in cases where a student has not yet submitted his synopsis.
(b) (i) If the synopsis of the Ph.D. thesis has been submitted before the supervisor proceeds on leave, he or she will continue to be the supervisor and only a caretaker supervisor will be appointed.
(ii) The Ph.D. thesis has been submitted before the supervisor proceeds on leave, he will continue to be the supervisor and only a caretaker supervisor will be appointed.
(iii) Further, if a major revision becomes necessary, and the sole supervisor is on leave, they should be asked to specifically state whether they would be able to effectively help the student carrying out the major revisions within a reasonable time. In case the sole supervisor expresses one‚Äôs inability to continue owing to some reason, the caretaker supervisor, provided they have offered the required help in carrying out the major revision, shall automatically be treated as Joint-Supervisor of that candidate.
(c) If a supervisor proceeds on leave for a period less than 12 months initially, but later extends the leave beyond 12 months, the above procedure will be followed. The extension granting authority will inform the SPGB Section accordingly.

II. A Supervisor Retires

A faculty member who is due to retire within the next two years can be appointed as a Joint-Supervisor and can continue to be the joint supervisor even after his retirement provided the DPGC is convinced of his availability/continued guidance to the student. In other cases, a faculty member on retirement may continue as (i) a Supervisor, if re-employed or appointed Emeritus Fellow or appointed through any other fellowship; (ii) a Joint-Supervisor, if the synopsis of the Ph.D. thesis has been submitted. Appointment of another Supervisor, if necessary, will be as per I (a); and caretaker Supervisor as per I (b).

III. A Supervisor resigns A new Supervisor will be appointed, if necessary, as per I(a), and a caretaker supervisor as per I(b).

IV. A Supervisor passed away

A new Supervisor will be appointed in consultation of the student, if necessary, on the recommendation of DPGC. V. Under special circumstances a student may allow to change his /her supervisor by the recommendation of DPGC and approval of the SPGB R.8 COMPREHENSIVE EXAMINATION R.8.1 Each student will be required to take a Comprehensive examination which will test the students' comprehension of their broad field of research, academic preparation and potential to execute the proposed research plan. The Comprehensive examination should be a combination of written & oral examination (Ph.D. thesis proposal presentation). The examination will be conducted by the SRC.

R.8.1. (i) The following procedures to be uniformly followed by all the Depts. in conducting the Comprehensive examination for Ph.D. Scholars:
(a) A student should first clear the requisite Ph.D. course work.
(b) The written examination should then be conducted by the Convener, SRC of the student within the guidelines laid down by respective DPGC.
(c) The syllabus of the written examination, approved by the SRC will be given to the candidate at least two weeks before the examination.
(d) The result of the written examination should be declared within two weeks.
(e) After the student has passed the written examination, the oral examination and research plan with state-of-the-art-Techniques (SOTA) (should cover the problem definition covering the research proposal) should be conducted within three months by the SRC of the student within the broad guidelines laid down by the respective DPGC. The notification for the same should be intimated both to student and the SRC minimum of two weeks before the examination.

R.8.2 The Student's Research Committee on the basis of the performance of the student in the examination will make one of the following recommendations:

(a) Written exam
(i) Passed
(ii) To reappear in the examination after a defined period of time specified by the SRC and/or after taking additional courses.
(iii) Failed, without any re-examination he/she leads to be terminated.
(b) Oral Exam and Research plan including SOTA Presentation
(i) Approved
(ii) Not approved, to reappear after a defined period of time (maximum of three more months) specified by the SRC. Failing which the registration may be terminated.
R.8.3 A student will be provided a maximum of two attempts to pass the comprehensive examination.
R.8.4 Full-time and off campus students must complete the comprehensive examination after minimum of 12 months and maximum before 24 months from the date of joining the programme, failing which their registration will stand cancelled automatically.

R.9. ELIGIBILITY FOR THE CANDIDACY FOR THE DEGREE R.9.1 Application for the candidacy of the Ph.D. Degree shall be made by the student to the SPGB on the approved form (Annexure 'B') soon after having passed the comprehensive examination.

R.9.2 A student shall be formally registered as a candidate for the Ph.D. Degree after (s)he has complied with the following:
(i) has completed his/her course work with a minimum CGPA of 7.0;
(ii) has submitted a research plan duly recommended by DPGC, and approved by SRC.

R.10 PERFORMANCE MONITORING

R.10.1 The academic/research progress of each student/candidate will be monitored either by DPGC/SRC or by its sub-committee. For this purpose, each candidate will be asked to submit a progress report at the end of each semester to his supervisor(s). On receipt of the progress report, the supervisor(s) shall arrange with SRC or its sub-committee for an evaluation (which includes presentation as the progress of his/her Ph.D. work), once in a semester. In the last semester of his/her work, the Synopsis (Ph.D. thesis draft) presentation may be considered as his/her final evaluation and no more separate evaluation is required.
R.10.2 The supervisor(s) will submit the progress report to DPGC signed by the scholars and forwarded by the supervisor(s) every semester with grading.
R.10.3 Overall 'S' grade is to be awarded during that semester if the progress is 'satisfactory'.
R.10.4 If the progress is 'unsatisfactory', overall 'U' grade is to be awarded and appropriate action shall be taken. On the appearance of an 'U' grade, a warning would be issued to the candidate.
R.10.5 If there are two consecutive 'U's in two semesters, the registration will stand terminated automatically. Subsequently, the assistantship (in case of regular scholar) of the candidate would be withheld.
R.10.6 A Special case may be considered, if the candidate is awarded with ‚ÄòEx‚Äô grade, then his/her tuition fees may be waived off depending on the SRC report for the next semester.
R.10.7 Like all other courses, the grades for DT 900 will be discussed in the Department as per semester schedule notified for this. After the discussion, the grades are to be sent to SPGB section or the equivalent body and entered like for any other course.
R.10.8 The above process will continue till the Synopsis submission.
R.10.9 The semester for the research work will be as follows: I Term: July to December; II Term: Jan. to June.
R.10.10 The Ph.D. thesis must be submitted within six months of acceptance of the Pre-Synopsis else the student has to resubmit the synopsis. Special cases can be dealt by the DPGC.
R.10.11 The academic calendar will inform two dates for the submission of progress reports.

R.11 MINIMUM REGISTRATION REQUIREMENT R.11.1 A candidate who has a B.Tech degree or its equivalent shall be required to be registered for the degree for a period of not less than three calendar years (36 months) from the date of his/her initial registration. In exceptional cases, the minimum period of registration may be reduced to two calendar years (24 months) with the approval of the Senate. For a candidate who has an M.Tech./M.Sc./M.A. degree or its equivalent the minimum period of registration shall be two calendar years (24 months)

R.11.2 The candidates of all categories shall normally submit their Ph.D. thesis within a period of five years from the date of their joining the Ph.D. Programme. However, as a special case, this limit may be extended to a maximum of seven years (on year basis) by SPGB forwarded by DPGC/SRC after which the registration shall stand cancelled automatically.
R.11.3 A candidate may be allowed to convert from full-time PhD to off campus PhD but after three years of completion of full-time PhD and clearing the comprehensive examination.

R.12 PRE-SYNOPSIS

R.12.1 Holding of Pre-Ph.D. seminar (Ph.D. thesis draft) is an essential requirement before the SRC considers the synopsis of a Ph.D. scholar. On completion of the research work, the candidate shall submit to DPGC through supervisor(s) approved by SRC, one hard copy and the soft copy of the same including the publications of research work. The Convener, DPGC will forward the synopsis with its recommendations to the SPGB. A panel of at least ten experts (at least in the rank of Associate Professor or equivalent Scientist grade) in the area would be suggested by the SRC, Convener while forwarding the title and synopsis of the Ph.D. thesis. The panel so recommended would include at least 40% of the examiners from outside India. Written e-mail concurrences from all the examiners are required.

R.13 BOARD OF EXAMINERS

R.13.1 On receipt of the title and synopsis of the Ph.D. thesis, the chairman of SPGB will choose three external examiners for each candidate from the DPGC recommended list. The Examiners‚Äô Board will consist of Internal Examiner(s) (normally the Supervisor(s)), and three External Examiners; and two will be from within India and one from abroad who shall be experts in the subject of the Ph.D. thesis. A person working in the same laboratory(ies)/Institution(s) where Research Scholar is employed (or to be employed) cannot be appointed as External Examiner for evaluating the Ph.D. thesis of that Research Scholar. Further no person can be appointed as External Examiner from the Laboratory (in collaboration) to which the Joint supervisor(s) of the Research Scholar belongs.
R.13.2 There is no bar in inclusion of name of examiner who was the ex-student of the guide or had received Ph.D. Degree of the Institute under his guidance at least 5 years earlier.

R. 14 PH.D. THESIS SUBMISSION

R.14.1 The Ph.D. thesis shall be written in English in the specific format (Annexure C) and shall contain a critical account of the candidate's research. A Ph.D. thesis may/shall be supplemented by published work, if necessary. No part of the Ph.D. thesis or supplementary published work shall have been submitted for the award of any other degree.
R.14.2 A candidate may submit his/her Ph.D. thesis within the time provided that:
(i) (S)he has completed the minimum period of registration
(ii) (S)he has become a candidate for the award of Ph.D. degree
(iii)
(iv) (S)he has submitted the title and synopsis of the Ph.D. thesis as approved by SRC during the time of Synopsis seminar.
(v) ((S)he has published one journal/conference in the allied areas of research as notified by the DPGC. DPGC can give also recommendation for the same
(vi) The candidate should submit a plagiarism report, which should approved by the supervisor and to be accepted by the Dean (Academic)

R.14.3 The candidate for the degree shall submit three copies of the Ph.D. thesis in a soft bound format and a soft copy in the form of compact disc/cloud upload.

In case of joint supervision four/five copies of Ph.D. thesis shall be submitted by the candidate. R.14.3 A nominal fees will be chargeable to the candidate for semester registration after submission of the thesis and the Oral Defense examination.

R.15 PH.D. THESIS EVALUATION

R.15.1 Each Examiner will be requested to submit to the Dean (Academic) a detailed assessment report and his recommendations on the prescribed Performa within two months of the date of receiving the Ph.D. thesis.
R.15.2 In the event that the Ph.D. thesis report is not received from an Examiner within a period of three months, the Dean (Academic) may appoint another examiner in his place for evaluating the Ph.D. thesis. Within three months, the examiner may be given reminders as deemed necessary.
R.15.3 Two reports out of three examiners are essential, in which one of them report should be from foreign examiner.
R.15.4 In the event of the candidate being required to submit a revised Ph.D. thesis, s(he) shall in the normal circumstances, submit the same within a period of six months from the date of communication in this regard from the Dean (Academic). However, in exceptional circumstances this period may be extended by the Dean (Academic) by another six months, the total revision time irrespective of the number of the revisions allowed, will not exceed a period of one year.

R.16 AWARD OF Ph.D. DEGREE

R.16.1 A candidate who has been recommended for viva-voce examination on the basis of Ph.D. thesis evaluation, shall be required to defend his/her work/Ph.D. thesis orally before a duly constituted committee hereinafter referred to as Oral Defense Committee (ODC) preferably during working hours of the Institute. Any exception to this, a prior approval from the Dean Academic may be taken.
R.16.2 The ODC shall consist of the Internal Examiner(s), and one External Examiner. If none of the External Examiners is available for the conduct of the Oral Defense, an alternative External Examiner shall be appointed by the SPGB for this purpose only.
R.16.3 On the completion of all stages of examination, the Oral Defense Committee shall recommend to the SPGB, one of the following courses of action:
(a) that the degree be awarded;
(b) that the candidate be re-examined at a later specified time in a specified manner;
(c) that the degree shall not be awarded;

In case of (a) and (b), the Oral Defense Committee shall also provide to the candidate a list of all corrections and modifications in the Ph.D. thesis (if required) including suggestions made by the Examiners during the Ph.D. thesis evaluation.

R.16.4 The Degree shall be awarded by the Senate, provided that:

(a) the Oral Defense Committee so recommends;
(b) the candidate produces a 'No Dues Certificate' in the prescribed form and gets it forwarded along with the report of ODC, and
(c) the candidate has submitted one soft copy of the Ph.D. thesis and two hard cover copies of the Ph.D. thesis; one for the Department's Library and one for the Central Library. Ph.D. thesis should incorporate all necessary/corrections/modifications.

R.17 FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE

R. 17.1 Those students who are admitted on full-time basis are considered, on request, for the award of Teaching/Research Assistantship for a minimum of 6-8 hrs/week with a scholarship as guided by MHRD. The maximum duration of which assistantship can be awarded to any Ph.D. student is 5 years or till the end of the semester in which Ph.D. thesis is submitted, whichever is earlier. Continuation of the Assistantship is contingent on satisfactory academic performance and satisfactory performance in the discharge of responsibilities (or Teaching Assistantship) assigned under the scheme. If performance in responsibilities (or Teaching Assistantship) is found not satisfactory his/her scholarship maybe stopped as an immediate effect as recommended by SRC.

R. 17.2 It is to be noted that admission to the Programme and award of Assistantship are not linked. Admission to any programme does not guarantee the award of assistantship. Those who are not awarded Assistantship can continue with the programme as without fellowship.
R. 17.3 After submission of the Ph.D. thesis, the scholar will not be provided any PhD assistantship further.
R. 17.4 If a student submit his/her Ph.D. thesis within four years (S)he can be given a assistantship of another maximum of six months. But after 5 years of enrollment then no assistantship will be given in any circumstances.
R.17.5. Externally funded PhD student can be given the institute assistantship after completion of the external fund on availability of the assistantship under project PI/supervisor but the total period of scholarship shall not exceed 5 years.

R.18 LEAVE & ATTENDANCE

(a) A full-time Ph.D. student, during his/her stay at the Institute will be entitled to leave for 30 days on pro-rata basis (2.5 days per month) and additional 10 days on medical grounds, per academic year. He/she will not be entitled to mid-semester breaks, summer and winter vacation at the end of the first semester.

Leave beyond 30 days in an academic year may be granted to a Research Scholar in exceptional cases, by the Head of the Department concerned, subject to the following conditions:

(i) the leave beyond 30 days will be without Assistantship/Scholarship; and
(ii) such an extension of up to additional 30 days will be granted only once during the programme of the Scholar.
(iii) the leave may be subject to the approval of the Head of Department/ Programme Coordinator concerned on the recommendation of the Supervisor; and a proper leave account of each scholar shall be maintained by the Department Coordinator concerned.

A woman research scholar will be eligible for Maternity Leave with Assistantship for a period not exceeding 135 days once during the tenure of the award (but not during the time of course work). This period shall be within the maximum limit of five years.

ATTENDANCE

A Ph.D. student irrespective of the source of research assistantship including self financing student not drawing any assistantship and sponsored student, while pursuing course work, must attend at least 80% of classes in each course in which they are registered. In case their attendance falls below 80% in any course they will be awarded with failed grade in that course. For the above purpose, if the number of classes works out to be a number which is not a whole number, the round off integer will be treated as the required 80% attendance.

R.19 WITHDRAWAL FROM SEMESTER/COURSES

Certification by a Registered Medical Practitioner shall be considered if the student makes a valid case for remaining absent from the Institute. The SPGB would be the deciding authority to allow the student‚Äôs withdrawal if it is found to be convinced at the latter‚Äôs inability to continue the Courses owing to unforeseen and uncontrollable reasons.
R.19.2 The Institute disallows withdrawal after the commencement of Major exams. The medical certificate justifying the student‚Äôs absence owing to ill health ought to be furnished to the Institute within two days of re-joining, in case it has not already been provided. There is no provision for a back-dated sanction of the student‚Äôs withdrawal.
R.19.3 The following factors would be pre-requites for accepting a request of withdrawal from the Course within 2nd minor tests held (but result is not declared) on the date before applying for withdrawal.
R.19.4 A student may be allowed to withdraw from a course by approval of the supervisor(s) with permission from DPGC within 10 days of starting of that semester.

R.20 CANCELLATION OF REGISTRATION

R.20.1 The Registration of a student shall stand cancelled upon due permission from the SPGB, under one or all of the conditions mentioned below:

(i) An uninformed/unsanctioned leave of absence for a period of four continuous weeks.
(ii) A student‚Äôs withdrawal from the PhD Program, either voluntarily or recommended by the DPGC with a minimum of 15 days notice from both sides.
(iii) The student‚Äôs failure to renew registration in a given semester, in keeping with the provisions of the Regulations and Processes
(iv) If the student does not attain qualifying marks/score/percentage in the Comprehensive exams
(v) Unsatisfactory academic performance of the student
(vi) A CGPA lower than 7.00 during any given time in the student‚Äôs coursework
(vii) If a case of termination based on relevant authorizing body forwards a student‚Äôs misconduct or indiscipline.

R.21 OFF-CAMPUS WORK

Off campus work for a PhD student may be permitted for a maximum period of 12 months.

R.21.1 In such cases on duty leaves may be permitted for a maximum of 12 months
R.21.2 The Scholarship can be continued provided that the attendance of the candidate will be recorded by the collaborator under whom the off campus works will be carried and he/she is not getting any scholarship from the collaborator/other sources.

ANNEXURE C

Regulations for preparation of Ph.D. thesis

1. Ph.D. thesis should be type-written on both sides of good quarto-size / A - 4 size paper in double space with sufficient margins with multiplastic ring binding.
2. Suitable reproduction of Indian-Ink diagrams should be used. Photographs should be suitably mounted on the same quality paper as the Ph.D. thesis.
3. Reference should be given in a style in the text consistent with a standard journal in the field.
4. Three copies of Ph.D. thesis in soft binding in white color cover must be submitted for evaluation. In case of student being supervised by more than one supervisor, appropriate number of additional copies must be submitted.
5. The cover should have the following printed on it in block letters:

(a) the title at the top (b) author's name in the middle (c) Name of the Department and Indian Institute of Technology Jammu at the bottom.

6. After the Viva-Voce Examination, two copies of Ph.D. thesis in hard maroon-color-cover must be submitted. The cover should have the material indicated in item 5 above. Besides, the following should be printed on the spine of the Ph.D. thesis:

(a) the year of publication at the top (b) the author's last name in the middle and (c) Ph.D. at the bottom.

7. The contents of the Ph.D. thesis should have the following format:

(i) Inner cover page (ii) Certificate of the Supervisor(s) (iii) Acknowledgements (iv) Abstract (v)Table of contents (vi) List of figures (vii) Body of the Ph.D. thesis (viii) References

(ix) Appendices and (x) Brief Bio-data of the author.
